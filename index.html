HOW IT WORKS

Spy camera lenses are designed to capture light, meaning they will reflect back concentrated, circular highlights when illuminated by an external source. Hidden Eye runs a localized image thresholding pipeline:

  • Adaptive Contrast: Isolates sudden local high-contrast peaks.
  • Intensity Isolation: Filters out dark shapes, keeping only specular highlights.
  • Geometric Verification: Checks circular ratios to ensure reflections are perfectly round.
  • Point Tracking: Tracks specific coordinate matches across frames to suppress random noise/flickers.

SCANNING INSTRUCTIONS

To maximize detection accuracy, use the following scanning protocol:

  1. Dim the Lights: Make the surrounding room as dark as possible to maximize reflection contrast.
  2. Excite the Lens: Hold your phone (or an external flashlight) directly next to the webcam. Sweep the light across the room.
  3. Calibrate Sliders: If you are getting false positives, increase the Min Brightness or Min Circularity slider.

PRIVACY COMPLIANT

100% On-Device Processing. This application does not upload your video stream or images to any server. All processing is executed locally in your browser sandbox using WebAssembly OpenCV modules.

PROTOTYPE DISCLAIMER

Hidden Eye is a proof-of-concept prototype. It is designed to demonstrate local browser-based computer vision (OpenCV.js) for detecting camera lens reflections and should not be used as a primary security measure.

Accuracy depends on environmental lighting, device camera quality, and proper calibration of sliders. For professional sweeps, dedicated RF or optical scanning equipment is recommended.
